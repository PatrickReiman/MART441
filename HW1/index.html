Why do people feel the need to pad explanations?

Often when someone is trying to explain a concept to someone who does not understand said concept they will write paragraphs upon paragraphs of text to try and get their point across. I feel this has the opposite effect as most people don't like to read huge blocks of text (like this one) and broken up chunks of text that have highlights (bold, italics, etc.) for important context would be much better. I think this mindset is a holdover from conservation as we often go off on tangents or talk about unrelated things when speaking, however, this doesn't work well in written text. If one is hell-bent on explaining things in big chunks of text the best way to do it is by explaining concepts that may be relevant to understanding a more complex topic first. An example could be that you need to explain the idea of voltage and current to someone before they can understand something like action potentials. So instead of just assuming they know the content, you first give a short, concise explanation of those concepts before you jump into the more complex topic.
